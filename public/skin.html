How to examine the skin

General observation

Inspect lumps, colour changes, distribution
Gently palpate for texture, temperature or tenderness

UV (Wood’s) light

In a blacked-out room check for characteristic fluorescence:
tinea (blue-green), pityriasis versicolor (coppery-orange), erythrasma (coral-red), vitiligo (blue-white), acne (orange-red), head lice nits (pale-blue)

Dermoscopy

Hold Arclight polariser like a pen, check: ABCDE (Asymmetry, Border, Colour, Diameter >6mm, Evolving)
Study: PDSBV (Pigment network, Dots, Streaks, Blue-white, Vascular)

Know your lesion: normal, suspicious, inflamed
